I want you to design a lean MVP (Minimum Viable Product) for a Daily Life Management and Study Progress Tracking System.
This is for serious personal academic improvement, but I do NOT want overengineering. The goal is simplicity, clarity, and fast execution.
Your task is to design the simplest version that:
* Tracks daily tasks
* Tracks study sessions
* Tracks subject-wise progress
* Tracks habits
* Allows weekly review
* Allows monthly progress tracking
* Provides basic performance insights
1️⃣ MVP Philosophy
Design this system using these strict rules:
* Keep it minimal
* Avoid unnecessary features
* Avoid complex architecture unless truly needed
* Focus on core functionality only
* Prioritize speed of development
* Make it easy to maintain
* Design for one primary user (me)
* Allow future expansion, but do not overbuild now
2️⃣ Core Features Only
A. Daily Page (MVP Version)
* Top 3 priorities
* Simple to-do list
* Study sessions (subject + duration + topic)
* Total study hours (auto-calculated)
* Habit checklist
* End-of-day reflection (short text)
B. Weekly Review (Simple)
* Total study hours
* Topics completed
* Weak subjects
* 3 wins
* 3 improvements
* Plan for next week
C. Monthly Overview (Simple)
* Total study hours
* Subject progress summary
* Habit consistency %
* One major insight
* Next month focus
3️⃣ Tech & Architecture Decisions
Now you must:
1. Decide if authentication/login is necessary for MVP.
2. Decide if a backend/database is required for MVP.
3. Suggest the simplest tech stack possible.
4. Explain why your choice avoids overengineering.
5. Describe how this can scale later if needed.
Important:
* Prioritize simplicity over scalability.
* Avoid complex systems unless absolutely required.
* Assume this is for personal use first.
4️⃣ Data Structure (Minimal)
Design only the essential data models needed for MVP. Avoid advanced analytics. Avoid complex relationships unless necessary.
5️⃣ Development Phases
Explain:
* What to build in Phase 1 (true MVP)
* What to delay to Phase 2
* What to ignore completely for now
The goal is clarity and execution speed.
Design something that can realistically be built quickly and used daily without friction.
Do NOT design a startup-level system. Design a disciplined personal productivity tool MVP.
